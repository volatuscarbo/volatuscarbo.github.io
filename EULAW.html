Verifier Impact Analysis — EU ETS / CORSIA / UK ETS

An impact assessment of regulatory requirements and recent amendments as they apply to accredited verifiers operating across EU ETS, CORSIA, and UK ETS aviation schemes. Impact levels: HIGH MEDIUM LOW

🏛️ Accreditation Requirements

▼
🇪🇺 EU ETS
HIGH

ISO 17029 + National Accreditation Body

Verifiers must be accredited by a NAB per Reg. 2018/2067 (AVR). Accreditation must cover the specific scope (aviation, stationary). Cross-border recognition via EA MLA applies.

MEDIUM

AVR Amendment 2025/1192

Latest amendment (June 2025) updates certain accreditation aspects. Verifiers must review whether their scope certificates remain valid under the new rules.

LOW

Accreditation Regulation EC 765/2008

General accreditation framework applies where AVR is silent. No direct action needed unless NAB issues new guidance.

🌍 CORSIA
HIGH

ISO 14065 Accreditation Required

CORSIA verifiers must be accredited under ISO 14065:2013 by a national accreditation body. Appendix 6, Section 2 of ICAO Annex 16 Vol. IV applies. Separate from EU ETS accreditation.

HIGH

ICAO Central Registry (CCR)

Verifiers must be listed in the CORSIA Central Registry. Member State competent authorities manage eligibility. Check CCR before each engagement.

MEDIUM

EU Implementation — Delegated Reg. 2025/927

The EU adopted ICAO CORSIA MRV rules via Delegated Reg. 2025/927 (May 2025). EU-based verifiers must understand the interface between CORSIA and EU ETS obligations.

🇬🇧 UK ETS
HIGH

UKAS Accreditation — Mandatory

Only UKAS-accredited verifiers are accepted. EU member state accreditations (e.g. COFRAC, DAkkS, RvA) are NOT recognised for UK ETS. Verifiers serving both schemes need dual accreditation.

HIGH

No Physical UK Presence Required

Verifiers do not need a UK registered office or physical presence — but UKAS accreditation is still mandatory. This creates scope for EU-based firms to hold both accreditations.

LOW

UKAS Maintains Verifier List

Operators must verify their verifier appears on the current UKAS list. Verifiers should ensure their scope certificate is current and covers aviation.

🔎 Audit & Verification Methodology

▼
🇪🇺 EU ETS
HIGH

Reasonable Assurance Standard

Verifiers must reach a reasonable assurance opinion. Strategic analysis, risk analysis, and verification plan required per AVR Articles 9–13.

HIGH

Non-CO₂ Aviation Monitoring (2024)

New monitoring rules (Aug 2024) for non-CO₂ effects (contrails, NOx) from aviation. Verifiers must develop competence in reviewing these new data streams for affected operators.

MEDIUM

Site Visits & Remote Verification

Risk-based approach to site visits. Remote verification tools permitted under specific conditions. Verifier must document rationale for approach chosen.

🌍 CORSIA
HIGH

Emissions Report + Procedure Conformity

CORSIA verification covers both the emissions data (reasonable assurance) and conformity of procedures with the regulation. Broader scope than EU ETS data-only verification.

HIGH

Verification Report Submission: 31 Jan

Verified Emissions Units Cancellation Report must be submitted by 31 January. Verifier must complete work and issue report well before this date to allow operator submission.

MEDIUM

SAF Methodology Changes (2025)

Updated CORSIA default LCA values and SAF methodology (June 2025). Verifiers must apply current values when reviewing SAF credit claims.

🇬🇧 UK ETS
HIGH

Activity Level Data Verification

Operators must submit a verification report for activity level data signed by a UKAS-accredited verifier. Small Emitters Tool (SET) reports also require independent verification.

MEDIUM

SAF Treatment Consultation

UK ETS SAF treatment consultation ongoing. Verifiers should monitor GOV.UK for finalised rules that will affect how SAF volumes are treated in verified emissions reports.

LOW

Operator Responsibility for Verifier Scope

Operators are responsible for ensuring their verifier is accredited for the correct scope. Verifiers should communicate scope clearly in engagement letters.

⚖️ Recent Regulatory Changes — Verifier Impact

▼

Directive 2023/958 restores full EEA scope for aviation ETS and phases out free allowances for aviation by 2026. Key verifier impacts:

  • Verifiers must understand the CORSIA/EU ETS interface — operators may have reduced EU ETS obligations for routes also covered by CORSIA offsetting.
  • Verification scope must correctly reflect which flights fall under EU ETS vs. CORSIA vs. both.
  • SAF incentive claims require documented evidence — verifiers must check sustainability certification (e.g. ISCC, RSB) is valid and in scope.
  • → Read Directive 2023/958

The June 2025 amendment to Reg. 2018/2067 is the most recent update to verifier obligations. Key impacts:

  • Review updated accreditation requirements with your NAB — scope certificates may need updating.
  • Check whether any changes to verification data requirements affect your current audit procedures and templates.
  • Update internal quality management documentation (QMS) to reflect new requirements before next verification cycle.
  • → Read Reg. 2025/1192

CORSIA Phase 1 began 1 January 2024. 126 ICAO Member States are participating. Key verifier impacts:

  • Verifiers must confirm they appear in the CORSIA Central Registry (CCR) before accepting mandates.
  • ISO 14065 accreditation is distinct from ISO 17029 (EU ETS). Both are needed for verifiers serving both schemes.
  • Verification covers procedure conformity in addition to emissions data — expand audit programs accordingly.
  • Offsetting requirement calculations use ICAO growth factor — verifiers must validate the operator's offsetting calculation methodology.
  • → ICAO CORSIA Portal

New monitoring rules for non-CO₂ aviation effects were agreed in August 2024. Key verifier impacts:

  • This is a monitoring/reporting requirement at present — not yet linked to the cap. However, verifiers should anticipate that verified non-CO₂ data will eventually feed into compliance obligations.
  • Build competence now in reviewing contrail and NOx monitoring data from airline operators.
  • Review what data operators are required to collect and ensure monitoring plans are updated accordingly.
  • → EC Announcement

✅ Verifier Audit Readiness Checklist

▼

Accreditation & Scope

  • ISO 17029 accreditation current and in scope for aviation (EU ETS)
  • ISO 14065 accreditation current and listed in ICAO CCR (CORSIA)
  • UKAS accreditation current with aviation scope (UK ETS)
  • Reviewed AVR 2025/1192 changes with NAB — scope certificate updated if needed
  • QMS documentation updated for latest AVR requirements

Audit Process

  • Verification plan covers EU ETS / CORSIA / UK ETS scope correctly
  • CORSIA/EU ETS interface assessed — correct flight categorisation confirmed
  • SAF sustainability certificates checked and in scope
  • Non-CO₂ monitoring data reviewed where operator is required to report
  • Operator's offsetting calculation methodology (CORSIA) validated
  • CORSIA verification report ready for operator submission by 31 January
  • Site visit rationale documented (or remote verification justification recorded)

Key Dates & Deadlines

Critical deadlines for operators and verifiers across EU ETS, CORSIA, and UK ETS for the current and upcoming compliance cycles.

📅 Annual Compliance Calendar

▼
31 January
CORSIA — Operator EUCR Submission
Verified Emissions Units Cancellation Report submitted to competent authority. Verifier must complete work significantly before this date.
28 February
EU ETS / UK ETS — Surrendering Deadline Notice
Operators receive final offsetting requirements (CORSIA). Verification reports for emissions data should be well underway.
31 March
EU ETS & UK ETS — Verified Emissions Report
Operators submit verified annual emissions report to competent authority. Verifier must issue final verification report before this date.
30 April
EU ETS — Allowance Surrender Deadline
Operators must surrender EUAs equal to verified emissions from previous year.
30 April
UK ETS — Allowance Surrender Deadline
UK operators surrender UKAs equal to verified emissions from previous year.
Q4 each year
Verifier Accreditation Review
Annual check: confirm NAB / UKAS scope certificates are current; review any regulatory changes that affect accreditation scope for the coming year.
